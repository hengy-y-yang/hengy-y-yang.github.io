Coming from motifs in the story

Overarching Themes

What are some motifs that contribute to the main idea?

IT'S IMPORTANT TO look past the scandal of any information to search for its root cause. Why do the characters act in such a way to make them look like fools in hindsight? Those are due to the motifs of the story that contribute to the theme. First, let us take a look at Darcy.

Propriety stemming from reputation can limit real freedom

DARCY'S UNMOVING PRIDE may look like all to his character at first, but we find out that not even his reputation can stand in the way of love. As we followed the news just like everyone else, we realized that we should not have vilified Darcy, and should have looked into his character further instead of instantly assuming it to be horrible just from his pride. We editors at Propriety have always prided ourselves on our accuracy in writing articles, so this is a big wake up call to make sure we look further before acting hastily.

Assumptions on someone's disposition typically result in awkward hostilities

NEXT, ELIZABETH'S PREJUDICE is just like ours. She judged Darcy on his history with Wickham, on things unrelated to him, you name it. Although the only thing she should have judged is him at the present, her presuppositions make it hard to do so. We harboured many of the same hostilities at Darcy, and although it is hard, we would like to make amends for him. In the future, we editors promise that we WILL analyze facts accurately, not just create interesting stories.
