Bingleys moving to Longbourn

AUTUMN 1

WORD GOT AROUND today that a new family just came to settle at Longbourn. They include Mr. Bingley, his sister Ms. Bingley, and his good friend Mr. Darcy.

Netherfield, a place deserted for ages, was looked over by Mr. Bingley, and he allegedly said it was a very fine house. He will surely settle into the estate before September 29th.

It has been rumored that Mr. Bingley earns upwards of 5 thousand pounds per year.

Dining with the Bingleys

AUTUMN 2

JUST TODAY, THE Bennets dined with Mr. Bingley and his companions at Netherfield Park. There were only five attendees on Mr. Bingley's side. Jane and Mr. Bingley started talking very soon after the Bennets arrived, and they very quickly fall in love.

One of our viewers say that Mr. Bingley was "good looking and gentlemanlike" (Austen 6). They also report that Mr. Darcy, a close friend of Mr. Bingley, was found out to "be proud, to be above his company, and above being pleased" (Austen 6).

Although everyone avoided Mr. Darcy after discovering his character, before the fact they admired him very much.

How Bad is Darcy's Pride?

AUTUMN 3

MR. DARCY'S PRIDE has revealed itself to be overwhelmingly strong. An inside person reports that Mrs. Bennet loved to "relate... the shocking rudeness of Mr. Darcy" (Austen 8). She said that "'he is a most disagreeable, horrid man[; s]o high and so conceited that there was no enduring him!'" (Austen 8).

An attendee of the dinner reported that between "[Bingley] and Darcy there was a very steady friendship, in spite of a great opposition of character" (Austen 10).

Why is Darcy such a good friend of Bingley? Their personalities are worlds apart, so they must have some other connection. Check back for updates!

The Par

AUTUMN 4

THE NETHERFIELD PARTY dined with Longbourn today. Most people had fun, although some of Mr. Bingley's relations ridicule the Bennet family.

Important news was just revealed by an insider, who says that Mr. Darcy has a fancy for Elizabeth! He was reported to have asked Eliza to dance during the party, but was soundly rejected on account of his prideful manners (Austen 17). Mr. Darcy, who stepped back but was not offended, said to Ms. Bingley that he "has been meditating on the very greate please which a pair of fine eyes in the face of a pretty woman can bestow" (Austen 17).

Later, Mr. Darcy reveals that he does admire Elizabeth. Could this be the blossoming of love? Tune in to find out.

The Stay at Netherfield

AUTUMN 5

YESTERDAY, JANE ARRIVED sick to Netherfield due to her travel in the rain. It is rumored that Mrs. Bennet sent her for a meeting at Netherfield on horseback instead of carriage so that she could not come back easily.

Elizabeth hurried to the Netherfield estate. She allegedly spent most of her time there with Jane, making sure she was well. An insider reported that Mr. Bingley's relatives ridiculed her greatly in her absence, but Darcy still admires her, saying her eyes were "'brightened by the exercise'" (Austen 24).

Mrs. Bennet went to Netherfield estate to check on her daughter this morning. Apparently she wants Jane to keep being sick so Jane can stay at Netherfield and create connections with its members.

The Netherfield Stay: Part 2

AUTUMN 6

DURING MRS. BENNET'S visit, an insider reports that she had no shortage of meanness to Mr. Darcy. While talking on the subject of "good breeding", Mrs. Bennet allegedly said that "'those persons who fancy themselves very important and never open their mouths, quite mistake the matter'" (Austen 29).

This is obviously a jab at Mr. Darcy, who Mrs. Bennet is trying to frame as someone too prideful for their own good. She also tries to frame Jane in a more favorable light, to encourage Mr. Bingley's feelings of regard toward her.

It has been rumored that Ms. Bingley also has feelings for Mr. Darcy. She tries to get Darcy's attention through many schemes, such as berating him with questions (Austen 31-32). She also tries to make his love seem imprudent by ridiculing Elizabeth's relations, to mostly no avail (Austen 35).

Mr. Collins Proposes to Elizabeth

AUTUMN 8

AN INSIDER HAS just informed that Mr. Collins, a clergyman many consider to be socially insensitive, has proposed to Elizabeth.

Elizabeth soundly rejected his proposal, but Mr. Collins, no doubt too eccentric to notice her refusal, keeps encouraging.

Elizabeth, frustrated at him, says "[i]f what I have hitherto said can appear to you in the form of encouragement, I know not how to express my refusal" (Austen 75). Mr. Collins, eventually convinced, decides to marry Charlotte. It is rumored that Charloote and Elizabeth are not good friends anymore.

The Late Mr. Darcy's Will was disregarded?

AUTUMN 9

Word just came in today that Mr. Darcy, in all of his selfishness, actually took away money from Mr. Wickham by disobeying his father's will!

Mr. Wickham's father was a steward for the late Mr. Darcy. Mr. Darcy was very fond of Wickham, and devoted a large part of his will to him.

However, Mr. Wickham exclaims that after the late Mr. Darcy's death Mr. Darcy actually stopped him from receiving the benefits. How someone could be so evil, who knows?
